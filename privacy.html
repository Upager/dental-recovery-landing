Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 8, 2024

1. Introduction

Dental Revenue Recovery ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and the privacy of your patients. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information when you use our software.

2. HIPAA Compliance

We are fully committed to HIPAA compliance. Our software is designed to ensure that Protected Health Information (PHI) remains secure and private at all times.

Key HIPAA Protections:

  • All software operations occur locally on your computer - no PHI is transmitted to external servers
  • We maintain a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with all customers
  • All data storage uses AES-256 encryption
  • Complete audit logs of all system activities
  • Automatic handling of patient opt-out requests

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Data Processed Locally on Your Computer:

  • Patient names and contact information (email addresses, phone numbers)
  • Treatment plan information (procedure codes, descriptions, fees)
  • Appointment scheduling data
  • Email communication records

Important: This data never leaves your computer. Our software operates entirely locally and does not transmit PHI to our servers or any third-party service.

3.2 Non-PHI Data We May Collect:

  • Practice name and contact information
  • Software usage statistics (anonymized)
  • Technical diagnostic information for support purposes
  • Billing and payment information

4. How We Use Information

4.1 Local Data Processing:

Patient data processed by the software on your computer is used solely for:

  • Identifying unscheduled treatment plans
  • Generating personalized recovery communications
  • Tracking recovery campaign effectiveness
  • Calculating revenue attribution for billing purposes

4.2 Non-PHI Uses:

  • Providing customer support
  • Processing billing and payments
  • Improving software functionality
  • Communicating product updates

5. Data Storage and Security

5.1 Local Storage:

  • All patient data is stored locally on your computer
  • Database files are encrypted using AES-256 encryption
  • Access is password-protected
  • Data is only accessible to authorized users on your network

5.2 Security Measures:

  • Read-only access to your PMS database (we never modify patient records)
  • Encrypted storage of all configuration and credentials
  • Secure SMTP connection for email transmission
  • Automatic session timeout after periods of inactivity
  • Regular security updates and patches

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or share patient data with third parties.

Limited Exceptions:

We may share non-PHI information only in the following circumstances:

  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or legal process
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets (with appropriate BAA transfer)
  • Service Providers: With vendors who assist in providing our services (all covered by BAAs)

7. Patient Rights

Your patients have the following rights regarding their data:

  • Opt-Out: Patients can opt out of recovery communications at any time
  • Access: Patients can request access to communications sent to them
  • Correction: Patients can request correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Patients can request deletion of their data from recovery campaigns

8. Email Communications

Compliance:

  • All emails include clear unsubscribe options
  • CAN-SPAM Act compliant
  • TCPA compliant (for SMS features)
  • Automatic opt-out list management

Email Security:

  • Emails sent through your own SMTP server
  • Sent from your practice's domain
  • No third-party email services with access to PHI
  • Secure TLS connection for transmission

9. Third-Party Services

Our software integrates with:

  • Your PMS: Read-only access to retrieve treatment plans and patient data
  • Your Email Server: For sending communications using your credentials
  • Payment Processors: For billing (Stripe/PayPal - PCI DSS compliant)

All third-party services that may access PHI are covered by Business Associate Agreements.

10. Data Retention

  • Local Data: Retained on your computer until you delete it or uninstall the software
  • Billing Records: Retained for 7 years as required by law
  • Support Records: Retained for 2 years
  • Opt-Out Records: Retained permanently to honor patient preferences

11. Your Responsibilities

As the practice owner/operator, you are responsible for:

  • Maintaining the security of your computer and network
  • Ensuring only authorized staff access the software
  • Using strong passwords and changing them regularly
  • Keeping the software updated with the latest security patches
  • Backing up your local database regularly
  • Honoring patient opt-out requests

12. Children's Privacy

Our software is designed for dental practices serving patients of all ages. Patient data for minors is processed with the same security and privacy protections as adult data. Communications to minors' guardians comply with all applicable regulations.

13. International Users

Our software is currently designed for use in the United States and operates in compliance with US healthcare regulations including HIPAA. If you are located outside the US, please contact us before using the software.

14. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website
  • Sending email notification to registered users
  • Displaying a notice in the software

Your continued use of the software after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

16. Business Associate Agreement

If you are a covered entity under HIPAA, we will execute a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with your practice upon installation of the software. Please contact us at thescwcapital@gmail.com to request a BAA.
